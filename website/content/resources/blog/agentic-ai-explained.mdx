## What is agentic AI?

Agentic AI refers to AI systems that can perceive their environment, make decisions based on goals and policies, and take autonomous action — with appropriate human oversight. Unlike traditional AI that responds to prompts, agentic AI operates continuously: monitoring signals, evaluating context, and executing tasks.

## How it differs from copilots

Copilots assist: they suggest code, draft text, or answer questions when asked. Agentic AI executes: it reads a new email, classifies intent, routes it to the right system, drafts a response, and logs the action — without waiting for a human to initiate each step.

The distinction matters for enterprises because copilots still require human attention for every interaction. Agentic AI handles routine operations autonomously and escalates only when judgment is needed.

## The three capabilities of agentic AI

1. **Perception**: Continuously monitoring signals from connected tools — new emails, updated records, filed requests, completed meetings.
2. **Reasoning**: Evaluating each signal against organizational policies, context, and approval rules to determine the right action.
3. **Action**: Creating records, drafting communications, routing approvals, and updating systems — with a complete evidence trail.

## Why enterprises are adopting it now

Three converging factors:

- **Data is everywhere**: Enterprises generate signals across dozens of tools. The volume is too high for manual processing.
- **LLMs enable reasoning**: Large language models provide the reasoning capability that makes policy-aware decision-making possible.
- **Governance is solvable**: Modern platforms can enforce permissions, approval gates, and audit trails — making autonomous action safe.

## What to look for in an agentic AI platform

- Agents scoped to specific business functions (not generic chatbots)
- Policy-aware decision making with configurable approval gates
- Complete audit trails linking every action to its triggering signal
- Human-in-the-loop controls for sensitive decisions
- Permission-safe execution that respects source-system access rules
